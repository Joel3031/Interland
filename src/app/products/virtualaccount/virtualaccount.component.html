The Foundation

What Is Virtual Account Management?

Virtual Account Management (VAM) is the backbone of modern corporate treasury. It lets businesses create digital sub-accounts — called virtual accounts — under a single physical bank account. Each virtual account acts as its own mini-ledger, giving companies precise control over receivables, disbursements, and balances across departments, customers, and geographies.

  • A virtual account is a sub-ledger of a real bank account
  • It carries a unique identifier for tagging and tracking payments
  • Businesses manage thousands of flows from a single real account
  • Opening is instant — no documentation, no waiting

Entity Management

Parent-Child Hierarchy for Every Business Structure

Assign and track virtual accounts based on your internal hierarchy — whether it's departments, regions, customers, partners, or vendors. Each account is tied to an alphanumeric identifier, keeping everything clean and organized.

COBO
Collection on Behalf Of

Each payer gets a unique VA for receivables — EMIs, utility payments, tax collections.

POBO
Payment on Behalf Of

The central account initiates payouts tagged to internal entities — vendor payments, payroll, disbursements.

Collection & Payment Models

COBO & POBO: How Money Moves

Virtual Account Management supports two primary fund flow architectures — choose based on whether your primary need is collecting from many or disbursing to many.

COBO

Unified Collections, Zero Reconciliation Chaos

In the COBO model, every payer — customer, dealer, or branch — gets their own dedicated virtual account. All payments route into a single physical account, but with clean, labeled trails for every entity. No guesswork, no manual mapping.

  • {{ point }}
FMCG Dealer Networks EMI Collections Public Utility Bills Insurance Premiums
POBO

One Treasury, Every Disbursement Tracked

In the POBO model, all payments originate from one central physical account. Each disbursement carries a virtual tag that identifies the source entity internally — without requiring separate accounts for every department or branch.
